SAMPLID: A new supervised approach for meaningful place identification using call detail records as an alternative to classical unsupervised clustering techniques

Manuel Mendoza-Hurtado, Juan A. Romero-del-Castillo, Domingo Ortiz-Boyer
2024
DOI

Abstract

This paper introduces SAMPLID (Supervised Approach for Meaningful Place Identification), a novel supervised method that addresses the challenge of identifying frequently visited and significant locations, such as home or work, using Call Detail Records (CDRs) from mobile phones. Unlike traditional unsupervised clustering techniques that group data based on inherent characteristics, SAMPLID employs a supervised learning framework. This approach utilizes a knowledge base specific to the problem, allowing for direct optimization towards specific place identification objectives. The study demonstrates SAMPLID's effectiveness using mobility data from Milan's mobile phone network, overcoming limitations of unsupervised clustering which may not always align with specific classification goals.
